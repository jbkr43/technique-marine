# Product

## Register

brand

## Users

Boat owners in the Cape Fear, NC area who need reliable mobile marine service — repairs, maintenance, electronics, plumbing, rigging — or a delivery captain to safely move their vessel. They arrive needing a solution, not browsing for inspiration. Some need urgent help; others plan ahead. Trust and competence are the baseline; the site needs to communicate "this person knows boats and shows up."

## Product Purpose

Technique Marine is a mobile marine service and delivery captain business. The website exists to convert local boat owners into clients by establishing credibility, showing service scope, and making contact frictionless. Success is a phone call or email from a qualified lead.

## Brand Personality

Rugged, Reliable, Local. Not polished-corporate, not overly nautical. The voice of someone who actually works on boats and has dirt under his nails. Confident without bluster. Expert without jargon. Coastal North Carolina character without being a caricature.

## Anti-references

- Generic corporate sites (banks, insurance, SaaS) — too cold and polished
- Overly nautical / themed — anchor clipart, rope borders, fish icons, boat-wheel motifs, navy-everything
- Outdated or cluttered marine sites — too much going on, no visual hierarchy
- "Nautical bank" look — navy suit version of a marine business, sterile and inauthentic

## Design Principles

1. **Show up.** The site should feel as direct and dependable as the service itself. No fluff, no filler sections.
2. **Built for the coast, not the cubicle.** Material, texture, and warmth drawn from working waterfronts — not resort kitsch.
3. **One call, done.** The path to contacting Dylan (phone, email) is always visible. Every section answers "why call Technique Marine."
4. **Earn trust in seconds.** The hero, services, and testimonials together need to convince a boat owner this is the right person, fast.
5. **Own the niche.** A small local business with a big service scope — the design should feel both capable and personal, not like a template stretched over a local business.

## Accessibility & Inclusion

WCAG AA best practices as default. No specific audience-mandated accommodations beyond standard modern web standards.
